This Is Lasagna, One of the most well-known food from Italy

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide pasta sheets with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and plenty of melted mozzarella. The meat sauce is typically made with ground beef or pork simmered in tomatoes, garlic, onions, and Italian herbs like oregano and basil. Each layer is built carefully, ensuring a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

Once assembled, the lasagna is baked in the oven until the top is golden and bubbling. It's then allowed to rest before serving, which helps the layers hold together. This hearty, comforting meal is perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers, as the flavors often deepen overnight.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • Crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Tomato sauce
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Lasagna noodles (either regular or no-boil)
  • Bay leaf (optional)

Step By Step Cooking Process

  1. Cook meat sauce – Sauté meat, onions, garlic, and tomatoes.
  2. Boil noodles – Cook lasagna sheets until al dente (or use no-boil).

  4. Layer – Alternate sauce, noodles, and cheese in a baking dish.
