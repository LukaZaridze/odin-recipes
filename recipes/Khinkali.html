This Is Khinkali, One of the most well-known food from Georgia

Khinkali is a traditional Georgian dumpling known for its juicy filling and unique shape. Typically filled with spiced meat—usually a mix of beef and pork—it can also be made with mushrooms, cheese, or potatoes. The dough is twisted at the top into a knot, forming a pleated pouch that holds flavorful broth inside.

Eating khinkali has its own etiquette: they are traditionally eaten by hand, holding the top knot and taking small bites to sip the juice before finishing the rest. The knot is usually left uneaten. Khinkali is not only a staple of Georgian cuisine but also a cultural symbol, often shared at family meals and festive gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Flour – for making the dough
  • Water – to mix with flour and form the dough
  • Ground meat – usually a mix of beef and pork (or lamb)
  • Onion – finely chopped, mixed into the meat for flavor
  • Black pepper – gives khinkali its signature spicy kick

Step By Step Cooking Process

  1. Make the dough – Mix flour, water, and a pinch of salt; knead until smooth and let it rest.
  2. Prepare the filling – Combine ground meat with finely chopped onions, water or broth (to create juicy filling), salt, and black pepper.
  3. Shape the dumplings – Roll out the dough, cut into circles, place filling in the center, and fold into pleats, twisting the top to seal.
  4. Boil the khinkali – Drop into salted boiling water and cook until they float and the dough is fully cooked (about 7–10 minutes).
  5. Serve hot – Remove with a slotted spoon and serve with black pepper; traditionally eaten by hand, holding the top knot.
